What is Classification?

Scientists classify living things into groups based on shared characteristics. This helps us:

  • Communicate clearly about species
  • Understand evolutionary relationships
  • Predict characteristics of unknown organisms
  • Identify and protect endangered species

The scientific name of an organism uses Genus and species (e.g., Homo sapiens for humans).

Rules for scientific names:

  • Written in italics (or underlined when handwritten)
  • Genus is capitalised; species is lowercase
  • Based on Latin or Greek

Part 4 â€” Measuring Biodiversity

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life in an area â€” both the number of different species (species richness) and how evenly they are distributed (evenness).

Simpson’s Diversity Index

$$D = 1 - \frac{\sum n(n-1)}{N(N-1)}$$

Where:

  • n = number of individuals of each species
  • N = total number of individuals
  • D ranges from 0 (no diversity) to 1 (maximum diversity)

A higher D = more diverse ecosystem
