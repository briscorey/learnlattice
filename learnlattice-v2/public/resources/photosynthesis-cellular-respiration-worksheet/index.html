Part 2 ‚Äî Limiting Factors of Photosynthesis

A limiting factor is the factor in shortest supply that controls the rate of a process. Even if you add more of everything else, increasing the limiting factor is the only thing that will speed up photosynthesis.

Three main limiting factors:

  1. Light intensity ‚Äî more light = more photosynthesis (up to a point)
  2. CO‚ÇÇ concentration ‚Äî more CO‚ÇÇ = more photosynthesis (up to a point)
  3. Temperature ‚Äî higher temp = faster enzymes (up to optimum, then enzymes denature)

Part 4 ‚Äî Cellular Respiration

The Equation

$$C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{ATP energy}$$

In words: Glucose + oxygen ‚Üí carbon dioxide + water + energy (as ATP)

ATP (adenosine triphosphate) is the cell’s “energy currency” ‚Äî it carries usable energy to where it’s needed.
